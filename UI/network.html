Frodo felt a strange certainty that in this matter Gandalf was right. His guess had been good. The Riders were looking for a hobbit, and they were far afield from the Shire; but he felt it was not he that they were seeking. They were looking for something that belonged to him and his enemy. No doubt they thought that he was just such another as the creature Gollum. He could see them clearly, riding like the wind in the moonlight, but now they were far ahead. Suddenly he knew in his heart that they were silently commanding him to wait. That he must wait. He cowered into the shadow of a large boulder, and waited.
